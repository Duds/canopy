## One-liner candidates

- 15 years translating strategy into executable service models for government and complex organisations.
- Service designer working where policy meets delivery — regulatory systems, multi-stakeholder services, government technology.
- Strategy that gets shipped: service design grounded in mining-grade systems thinking and government delivery.

## Positioning

- **Who I work with:** Australian Government departments, regulators, and complex private-sector organisations with safety-critical or compliance-critical operations.
- **What I do:** Translate policy and strategy into executable service models. Design regulatory services, multi-stakeholder systems, and the technology that supports them.
- **What I'm not:** A pure UX designer, a tech-first consultant, or a slide-deck strategist.

## Origin / why this work

- Started in mining — critical-control thinking shaped how I design for resilience, risk, and real-world failure.
- Moved into service design via technology delivery (5 years on Microsoft platforms).
- Now operating where the three meet: policy intent · service mechanics · technology enablement.

## Philosophy

> People, process, and technology are all imperfect — design must account for this reality.

- Systems thinking over surface UX
- Technology serves the service, not the other way around
- Critical-control mindset: design for failure, not happy paths
- Strategy isn't real until someone can run it
