Summary

The difference is not complexity. The slop patterns are often technically simpler than the intentional ones. The difference is intent.

AI defaults to patterns that were common in training data — rounded corners, gradient text, card zooms, three-column grids. These patterns are not wrong in isolation. They are wrong when they appear without decision.

Intentional design means every choice has a reason. The border shift signals interactivity without lifting. The asymmetric grid creates visual tension. The solid color is confident. The flat nav is grounded. Nothing is default.
