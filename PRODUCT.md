# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three distinct audiences, each arriving with different intent:

- **Potential client** — evaluating fit for regulatory, government, or industrial-domain engagement. Arriving from LinkedIn, tender lists, or referrals. Cares about outcomes, not credentials. Wants evidence of relevant problem-solving in their domain.
- **Potential employer** — assessing depth of practice and breadth of capability. Cares about narrative coherence across case studies. Wants to understand how Dale thinks and works.
- **Peer / collaborator** — designer, strategist, or technologist considering partnership. Cares about methods, worldview, and intellectual rigour. Wants authentic voice, not positioning.

## Product Purpose

Canopy is Dale Rogers' personal brand surface. It showcases service design and strategy work across government, regulated industry, and industrial contexts. The portfolio demonstrates problem-solving capability, domain knowledge, and collaborative practice to clients, employers, and peers. Success means visitors land with confidence that Dale understands their domain and can deliver outcomes.

## Brand Personality

Pragmatic. Problem-solver. Knowledgable. Approachable. Collaborative. Humble.

Not: elite, academic, arrogant, theoretical, condescending.

## Emotional Tone

Confidence and curiosity. Visitors should feel that this person knows their craft, has solved real problems, and is genuinely interested in understanding the next one.

## Anti-references

Explicitly avoid:
- Formulaic consultant templates (e.g., Webflow Agency template, Cargo, Are.na clones)
- Wordpress themes or drag-drop builders
- Corporate Memphis illustration style
- Trendy aesthetics chasing design trends
- Buzzword-heavy copy ("leverage", "synergy", "ecosystem")
- Post-it note theatre, whiteboard-pointing photos, expensive-desk-with-Macbook imagery
- Design as art posing (self-indulgent showcase over clarity)
- People-in-photos as metaphor for "collaboration" or "innovation"

## Accessibility

WCAG AA compliance required across all pages.

## Strategic Principles

1. **Evidence over credentials.** Show work and outcomes, not titles or years.
2. **Domain clarity.** Make it obvious what Dale works on (government, regulated industry, industrial, enterprise strategy) so the right people find the right case studies.
3. **Intellectual honesty.** Articulate problems clearly; don't oversell solutions.
4. **Approachable depth.** Rigorous thinking should be readable and human, not academic or gatekept.
